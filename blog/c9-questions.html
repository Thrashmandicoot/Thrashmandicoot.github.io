Being Specific

Asking the right questions involves asking a question in a way that solicits the answer that you want.

While the idea of asking for what one may want may seem simple, in practice, it's a lot harder to do. Here's a quick and dirty breakdown of key points to a good question.

  1. Be Specific ie:
    1. What is the impact of your problem?
    2. Where in your code are you having the problem?
    3. What errors are showing up when your code runs?
    4. What specifically are you trying to accomplish?

Basically, cut the question down to the most basic step you need to accomplish to make your code run. if you're starting a loop, and you have a particular variable that isn't updating, share an example of that loop that's failing, showcase that you've attempted to diagnose the problem yourself, perhaps with a printed statement of the variable that isn't updating, talk about solutions you've tried that have failed to resolve the problem.

Adding these types of strategies should help out your question asking overall.
