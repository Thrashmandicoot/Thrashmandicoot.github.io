A brief look into a big problem

Security in Tech

Years of advances in technology from web application development, to new ways of doing things has left many companies ill equiped to deal with the rising costs of maintaining legacy infrastructure. Lets first take a look at the risks involved.

The Risks

Having out of date legacy applications creates security vulnerabilities. First, consider the lack of developers maintaining applications, with less developers being hired on to maintain the code, means more ability for hackers and various malicious entities to gain access to sensitive data. With newer technologies and the rise in smart phones, it also means that employees of those companies can pose potential risks and increase potential leaks of sensitive data.

An easy example of this can be seen if you look into what happened to Sony pictures when many of their movies, that hadn't even been exposed in previews, got leaked, allegedly from North Korea, but others speculated it could have been an inside job. Either way showcases the above mentioned risks.

The Costs

Unfortunately, upgrading infrastructure is it's own beast. You can either pay developers to maintain, or build a new application and bridge the older data. Both methods cost a lot of resources, and while the most viable method is to upgrade the systems, the costs for doing so are often monumental especially if the company has been around a long time.

This is just a brief look at one of the bigger problems facing the IT world. While many companies are taking steps to bring their applications up to date there will be many that won't, and the management of risk will only play an increasing role in the future.
